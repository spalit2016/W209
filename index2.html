Traffic Collision Patterns of New York City

Traffic collisions

The visualizations below are based on NYPD's(New York Police Department) dataset of every collision in NYC by location and injury from 2013 to 2018. NYPD started collecting this data to comply with the local law #11 passed by NYC council in 2011. This data is manually run every month and reviewed by the TrafficStat Unit before being posted on the NYPD website. Each record represents a collision in NYC by city, borough and cross street.

The visualizations presented here aim to minimize vehicle collisions in New York City by providing relevant insights to general New Yorkers, commuters, city planners, traffic police officials and DMV officials to help promoting commuter awareness and planning better traffic management.

Collisions over Time

Yearly Trends

Following Chart shows the accident breakdown in the five NY boroughs over the years from 2013-2018. When hovering over the bars, the tooltips provide info such as # and % of collisions and fatal collisions in that borough in that year. You can interact with the additional filters such as vehicle type and contributing factors to understand how these factors influence the Yearly collision patterns.

Brooklyn has highest number of collisions among boroughs, followed by Queens. Interestingly, collisions involving bicycle where high in 2016 and 2017, whilst collisions involving bus was lowest in the same years. Additionally, collisions involving box truck were high in 2018. In terms of contributing factors, “Fatigue/Drowsiness” was recorded high in 2013 to 2015 while conversely “Following too closely” was higher in the years 2016-2018. “Backing unsafely, driver inattention and failure to yield right” are increasingly attributed to collisions over the years.

Monthly Trends

Following line graph depicts the collision pattern over the 12 months in a year. You can interact with additional filters such as year, vehicle type and contributing factors to understand how these factors influence the Monthly collision patterns.

Interestingly, bicycle collisions are highest in Brooklyn followed by Manhattan and they peak during summer months. Collisions involving taxi are much higher in Manhattan when compared to other boroughs. Collisions for all boroughs picked in May while February is the month with lowest collisions , presumably because of lesser number of days.

Hourly Trends

Following line graph depicts the collision pattern over the 24 hours in a day in different boroughs. You can interact with additional filters such as year, vehicle type and contributing factors to understand how these factors influence the hourly collision patterns.

Many of the contributing factors indicate higher collisions in the evening hours indicating driver inattention and fatigue is high during these times.Overall Collisions peak at 4 PM, not surprising as it is part of the rush hour.

This insight shows that traffic collision patterns in New York is in sync with the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration’s analysis that human error is the leading cause of all the accidents in US.

The  automobile manufacturers and tech companies are already taking major initiatives to develop self-driving technology as a solution to help reduce collisions and accident-related injuries. But full autonomy which works seamlessly for all use cases , at all streets at all weathers and at scale would be years if not decade away. It would be important for city planners and dmv officials to ensure that self driving technology is rolled out in safest manner possible and at places where it would have max impact in bringing down count of accidents.

By analyzing the impact of human errors in number of collisions and fatal collisions as well as identifying the most dangerous boroughs, zip codes and cross streets, city planners can make informed decisions about how and where to roll-out the self driving cars.
